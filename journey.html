My Journey

From my engineering foundation in India to my graduate studies and professional development in the UK, this timeline reflects a journey of learning, growth, and confidence — a path built one meaningful step at a time.

2018 – 2022 · India

Engineering Foundation

I completed my Bachelor’s in Aeronautical Engineering at Sathyabama University, Chennai, India. These four years taught me discipline, technical problem-solving, and teamwork. My final project involved developing a 2U Nano Satellite for atmospheric sensing, which enhanced my understanding of real-world engineering applications and collaboration.

I also participated in technical workshops and seminars, sparking my curiosity about how systems are designed, optimized, and managed.

2023 – 2024 · United Kingdom

Master’s in Engineering Management

In January 2023, I began my Master’s in Engineering Management at the University of Greenwich. The program helped me integrate engineering and management perspectives to understand operations, leadership, and planning.

My dissertation focused on “Ethical Considerations on the Use of AI in Healthcare Research.” This work refined my analytical skills and taught me the importance of responsibility and ethics in technology.

I completed my degree in August 2024 and attended my graduation ceremony in November 2024 — a proud personal milestone that symbolized the completion of a transformative chapter.

2024 – 2026 · Professional Growth

Career Building, Networking & Certifications

After graduation, I focused on gaining practical exposure through warehouse and logistics roles, learning the importance of accuracy, process improvement, and teamwork in high-paced environments.

From March 2025 to January 2026, I attended over 11 professional networking events — building meaningful connections, improving communication, and growing my confidence in professional spaces.

Alongside networking, I completed several certifications that strengthened my theoretical foundation:

Supply Chain Foundations

Inventory Management Foundations

This period helped me bridge academic knowledge with industry practice — preparing me for entry-level roles in logistics and supply chain management.
